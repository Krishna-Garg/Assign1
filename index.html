Gateway of India

The Gateway of India is an iconic arch-monument located in Mumbai, Maharashtra. It was built to commemorate the landing of King George V and Queen Mary in India on December 2, 1911. The monument, completed in 1924, stands 85 feet (26 meters) tall and is designed in the Indo-Saracenic architectural style, which combines elements of Indian, Islamic, and British architecture.

Golden Temple

The Golden Temple, also known as Harmandir Sahib or Darbar Sahib, is the holiest shrine in Sikhism. Located in Amritsar, Punjab, it was built in the late 16th century by Guru Arjan Dev, the fifth Sikh Guru. The temple is renowned for its stunning architecture, featuring a gold-covered sanctum and a serene pool called the Amrit Sarovar.

Qutab Minar

The Qutab Minar, also spelled Qutb Minar or Qutub Minar, is a towering minaret and “victory tower” located in the Mehrauli area of South Delhi, India. Standing at 72.5 meters (238 feet) tall, it is one of the tallest minarets in Asia12. The construction of this UNESCO World Heritage Site began in 1199 by Qutb-ud-din Aibak and was completed by his successor, Iltutmish, around 1220.

Red Fort

The Red Fort, also known as Lal Qila is a historic fort in Delhi, India, that historically served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors. Emperor Shah Jahan commissioned construction of the Red Fort on 12 May 1639, when he decided to shift his capital from Agra to Delhi.

Victoria Memorial

The Victoria Memorial is a grand marble building located in Kolkata, India. Built between 1906 and 1921, it was dedicated to Queen Victoria, the Empress of India from 1876 to 1901. This majestic structure stands amidst 64 acres of gardens and is now a museum under the Ministry of Culture.

India Gate

India Gate, monumental sandstone arch in New Delhi, dedicated to the troops of British India who died in wars fought between 1914 and 1919. India Gate, which is located at the eastern end of the Rajpath (formerly called the Kingsway), is about 138 feet (42 metres) in height.

Ajanta and Ellora

The Ajanta and Ellora Caves, located near Aurangabad in Maharashtra, India, are renowned for their stunning rock-cut architecture and intricate sculptures. The Ajanta Caves, dating from the 2nd century BCE to the 6th century CE, are primarily Buddhist and feature exquisite paintings and carvings depicting the life of Buddha. The Ellora Caves, on the other hand, span from the 6th to the 11th centuries CE and showcase a unique blend of Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain monuments. Both cave complexes are UNESCO World Heritage Sites and are celebrated for their artistic and architectural brilliance.

Hava Mahal

In keeping with the visual language of the “Pink City” of Jaipur, the Hawa Mahal is built entirely in red sandstone, which in the sunlight glows with a pink hue. It is often identified as the world's tallest building constructed without a foundation.
